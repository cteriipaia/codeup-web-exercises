National Parks FAQ

How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2018: 318,211,833.
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: More than 20,000
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the largest national park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres.
What is the National Park Service budget?
Fiscal Year (FY) 2014 Enacted: $2.98 billion.
Can I bring my pet to a national park?
Some national parks welcome pets—in developed areas, on many trails and campgrounds, and in some lodging facilities.
How do I apply for a job with the National Park Service?
National Park Service jobs, including both permanent and seasonal positions, are listed on USAJOBs.
What do I need to know about driving off road in national parks?
Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.

4 Fun Facts about 3 National Parks in Texas

San Antonio Missions National Historical Park

  • Fact 1: San Antonio Missions National Historical Park is located in San Antonio, Texas.
  • Fact 2: The park preserves and showcases four 18th-century Spanish colonial missions: Concepción, San José, San Juan, and Espada.
  • Fact 3: These missions were established by Franciscan friars to spread Christianity and convert the indigenous peoples of the region.
  • Fact 4: The park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its cultural and historical significance in representing the Spanish influence in the Americas.

Palo Alto Battlefield National Historical Park

  • Fact 1: Palo Alto Battlefield National Historical Park is located near Brownsville, Texas, commemorating the first major battle of the Mexican-American War that took place on May 8, 1846.
  • Fact 2: The park preserves the site of the Battle of Palo Alto, where forces led by General Zachary Taylor engaged Mexican forces under General Mariano Arista, marking the beginning of the conflict between the United States and Mexico.
  • Fact 3: Established in 1991, the park covers over 3,400 acres and includes a visitor center with exhibits, a film, and educational programs to help visitors understand the historical significance of the area.
  • Fact 4: Palo Alto Battlefield National Historical Park serves as a place for both historical reflection and outdoor recreation, offering trails, picnic areas, and opportunities for birdwatching amidst the battlefield's natural landscape.

Lyndon B. Johnson National Historical Park

  • Fact 1: Lyndon B. Johnson National Historical Park is located in the Texas Hill Country, encompassing the birthplace, boyhood home, and ranch of the 36th President of the United States, Lyndon B. Johnson.
  • Fact 2: The park is divided into two units: the Johnson City Unit, focusing on LBJ's early years and the LBJ Ranch Unit, which showcases the "Texas White House" where President Johnson conducted official business during his presidency.
  • Fact 3: Visitors to the park can explore the LBJ Ranch, which includes the President's reconstructed birthplace, the Johnson family cemetery, and the Texas White House, providing insights into the personal and political life of Lyndon B. Johnson.
  • Fact 4: Established in 1969, the park serves as a living memorial to Lyndon B. Johnson, preserving the cultural and historical landscape associated with his life and presidency, and offering educational programs to enhance public understanding of this significant period in American history.
